Coursework

Freshman Year


ENGR 1281.01H - Fundamentals of Engineering Honors 1
Exploring many engineering disciplines through targeted labs. Learning and practicing technical writing with professional lab reports. Working in teams to accomplish lab tasks, and for several group lab reports. Learning engineering ethics. Learning essential MATLAB and Excel skills. Learning C and C++. Programming a microcontroller with C++, including a final project to create a touchscreen-based game.

CHEM 1910H - Honors General Chemistry 1
Learning essentials of chemistry. Learning and practicing chemistry techniques in lab. Writing lab reports, further practicing technical writing skills. Learning to read scientific literature.

MATH 1172 - Engineering Math
Calculus-based class with an emphasis on engineering applications.

ENGR 1110.04 - Chemical Engineering Survey 1
Information about careers and educational opportunities in chemical engineering.

ENGR 1282.02H - Fundamentals of Engineering Honors 2, Nanotechnology
Worked with three team members to perform two project in parallel. Investigated the effects of low pH on yeast cell adhesion within microfluidics channels in a biocompatible material (PDMS). Performed theoretical research, design, and 3D-modeling of a lab-on-a-chip device intended to detect and diagnose Celiac Disease through automated analysis of a drop of blood. Learned technical drawing skills. Learned CAD through use of Solidworks.

CHEM 1920H - Honors General Chemistry 2
Worked with two team members to investigate the double perovskite Cs3SbX6, where X is a halide: Cl, Br, or I. Performed solid-state synthesis, characterized the product with powder X-ray diffraction and UV-Visible spectroscopy, and fabricated solar cells from our product to test efficiency under a solar light simulator. Continued learning of essentials of chemistry, and practice with reading scientific literature. Included short introduction to basic organic chemistry.

MATH 2177 - Math Topics for Engineers
Calculus, linear algebra, and differential equations, focusing on applications for engineering. Studied wave motion and heat flow.

ENGR 1120.04 - Chemical Engineering Survey 2
Information about careers and educational opportunities in chemical engineering.

Sophomore Year


CBE 2200 - Process Fundamentals
Mass and energy balance.

CHEM 2510 - Organic Chemistry 1
Essential organic chemistry concepts.

SOCIOL 3302 - Technology and Society
General education: The impact of new technologies on society throughout history, and how technology will continue to shape society in the future.

EDUTL 2368 - Introduction to Children's Literature
General education: The impact of conscious and subconscious ideology in media on childhood development, with an emphasis on close-reading techniques.

ARTEDUC 1600 - Art and Music Since 1945
General education: The development of modern art and music over time, factors contributing to this art evolution, and analysis of art and music.

CBE 2523 - Separation Process
Studying mathematical solutions for many unit operations, with an emphasis on separations. Includes evaporation, drying, distillation, absorption, adsorption, liquid-liquid extraction, leaching, stripping, filtration, and membrane-based separations.

CBE 2420 - Transport Phenomena I
Studying momentum, heat, and mass transfer at molecular, microscopic, and macroscopic levels. Particularly emphasizes using momentum balance to solve velocity and shear stress profiles and related properties of fluids under laminar flow, culminating in the derivation of the Equation of Continuity, the Equation of Motion, and the Navier-Stokes equation.

CHEM 2520 - Organic Chemistry 2
More advanced organic chemistry concepts, including aromaticity, aldehydes, ketones, carboxylic acid derivatives, amines, enolates, and other functional groups.

PHYSICS 1251 - Electricity, Magnetism, Optics, and Quantum Mechanics
Calculus-based physics exploring the fundamental mathematical concepts behind electricity, magnetism, optics, and quantum mechanics, with a particular emphasis on the derivation and use of Maxwell's equations.
